齐白石 Qi Baishi

Master of Chinese Painting (1864-1957)

The Artist

齐白石 (Qi Baishi) was one of China's most renowned painters of the 20th century. Born to a farming family in Hunan Province in 1864, he began his career as a carpenter before dedicating himself to art.

Known for his whimsical, vibrant paintings of shrimp, fish, crabs, frogs, insects, and flowers, Qi developed a distinctive style that blended traditional techniques with a modern sensibility. His works are characterized by bright colors, bold brushstrokes, and a perfect balance between realism and abstraction.

Qi Baishi's artistic philosophy can be summarized in his famous words: "Paintings must be something between likeness and unlikeness." This approach allowed him to capture the essence of his subjects rather than merely their outward appearance.

By the time of his death in 1957, he had produced thousands of paintings and earned recognition as one of China's greatest artists. His works now hang in major museums worldwide and continue to influence contemporary Chinese art.
